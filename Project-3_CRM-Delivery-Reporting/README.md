## â— Problem Statement
As SaaS organizations scale, CRM systems become central to managing customer onboarding,
internal workflows, and ongoing service delivery. However, delivery teams often lack
clear visibility into work allocation, resource utilization, and delivery progress
across CRM-related activities.

From a PMO perspective, the absence of structured reporting and allocation tracking
results in:
- Uneven workload distribution across teams
- Limited visibility into workforce utilization (WFM)
- Delays in identifying delivery risks and dependencies
- Inconsistent status reporting to stakeholders
- Reactive decision-making due to lack of consolidated delivery data

This creates challenges for delivery planning, prioritization, and stakeholder communication.

---

## ðŸŽ¯ Objective
The objective of this project is to establish a **structured PMO delivery support framework**
for CRM-related initiatives within a SaaS platform by improving visibility into:

- Resource allocation and workload distribution
- Workforce utilization and capacity constraints
- Delivery progress, risks, and dependencies
- Effort tracking to support budget and cost visibility at a PMO level
- Management-ready reporting for informed decision-making

The focus is on **delivery coordination and reporting**, not CRM system configuration
or ownership.
