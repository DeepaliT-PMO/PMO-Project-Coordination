Product Background

Wavpe is an NFC-enabled digital business card solution designed to modernize professional networking. The product combines a physical NFC business card with a mobile and web application that allows users to create, manage, and share their professional identity digitally.

By enabling instant contact sharing through NFC tap or QR code, Wavpe eliminates the limitations of traditional paper business cards while allowing real-time profile updates and a consistent digital presence across devices.

Why? (Objective)

For business.

Wavpe aims to replace traditional paper business cards with a scalable, digital solution that strengthens brand positioning and enables long-term product growth. The platform supports recurring engagement through editable profiles, reduces dependency on physical materials, and opens opportunities for future enterprise adoption and premium feature monetization.

For users.

Wavpe provides professionals with a faster, smarter, and more reliable way to share contact information. Users can maintain an up-to-date professional identity, avoid reprinting cards, and present a modern digital impression during business interactions.

How do we measure success?

Associated Objective and Key Results (OKRs)

  • The primary objective is to enable seamless, reliable, and high-quality digital contact sharing using NFC technology.
  • Key results include achieving a high NFC card activation success rate, ensuring reliable profile access through NFC and QR, and reducing reliance on traditional paper business cards for active users.

Success Metrics

Success will be measured through NFC card activation success rate, profile update success rate, profile page load performance, number of successful NFC and QR profile views, and overall user adoption following card activation.

Guardrail/Do not disturb metrics (These metrics shouldn’t be negatively affected)

The implementation should not negatively impact application stability, performance, or security. App crash rates must remain within acceptable thresholds, profile load times should not degrade, NFC tap failures must remain minimal, and user data privacy and security standards must be preserved.

Who are the users:

User Persona

  • The primary user persona is a working professional who frequently attends meetings, conferences, and networking events and requires quick, reliable contact sharing. This user values professionalism, convenience, and consistency.
  • The secondary persona is a sales executive who shares contact information frequently and needs the flexibility to update details, add social links, and ensure reliable access during high-volume interactions.

User Problems and Needs

Needs
Users need a fast and effortless way to share professional information and the ability to update details without reprinting physical cards.

Problems
Traditional business cards become outdated quickly, are easily misplaced, and offer no way to track or control how information is shared. Users lack flexibility and visibility after exchanging contact details.

Jobs to Be Done
Users want to share their professional identity instantly, ensure shared information is always current, and leave a lasting digital impression during networking interactions.

Evidence and Research

These problems are validated through feedback from professionals and sales teams, observations from networking and business events, industry trends toward contactless and digital solutions, internal product discovery discussions, and increasing adoption of NFC-based smart card technologies.

Solution:

  1. Brief of the solution.
  • Wavpe provides a hybrid solution that combines a physical NFC-enabled business card with a digital profile management platform. Each NFC card is uniquely linked to a user account, allowing instant access to the user’s digital profile through a simple tap or QR code scan.
  • Users can create and manage their professional information through the Wavpe app, including contact details, company information, and social links. Any updates made to the profile are reflected immediately, eliminating the need to reprint physical cards. The solution ensures a seamless, contactless, and modern networking experience across devices.
  1. Other alternatives are considered with prioritization metrics.

Multiple alternatives were evaluated before finalizing the Wavpe solution:
Alternative 1: QR-Code–Only Digital Business Card
This approach allows users to share profiles using QR codes without physical cards. While cost-effective, it relies on manual scanning and does not provide the same seamless or premium experience as NFC-based sharing.
Alternative 2: Mobile App–Only Contact Sharing
This solution depends entirely on app installation and user interaction on both sides. Adoption friction is higher, and sharing is less intuitive during quick networking interactions.
Alternative 3: Static NFC Card Without Editable Profile
This option allows NFC-based sharing but does not support real-time profile updates. Any change in details would still require card replacement, defeating a key user need.

Prioritization Rationale
The final solution NFC card combined with a dynamic digital profile was prioritized based on the following criteria:

  • User experience simplicity and speed
  • Flexibility to update information in real time
  • Reduced friction during networking interactions
  • Long-term scalability for enterprise and premium use cases
  • Strong differentiation from traditional and QR-only solutions
    This approach best balances usability, scalability, and long-term business value.

Product flow (Details of the feature/product):

Customer Journey

The customer journey for Wavpe begins when a user receives an NFC business card and installs or accesses the Wavpe app.

  1. The user signs up or logs into the Wavpe app.
  1. The user activates the NFC business card by linking it to their profile.
  1. The user creates or updates their digital business profile with professional details.
  1. During networking, the user shares their profile via NFC tap or QR code.
  1. The recipient views the digital profile and saves contact details.
  1. The user can update their profile anytime, and changes reflect instantly without reactivating the card.
    This journey ensures minimal friction, instant sharing, and long-term usability.

User Stories + User acceptance criteria

User stories define the functional requirements from the user’s perspective.

User Story 1: Digital Identity Exchange via NFC (Core Value Proposition)

As a business professional (individual user),

I want to instantly share my verified digital business profile using an NFC-enabled Wavpe card or QR,

So that I can establish professional connections seamlessly without manual data entry or physical card exchange.

Business Value

  • Eliminates friction in networking
  • Improves first-touch conversion in professional interactions
  • Positions Wavpe as a modern, sustainable alternative to paper cards

Acceptance Criteria

  • User can tap NFC card or scan QR to open their Wavpe profile
  • Profile opens without requiring the recipient to install the app
  • Contact details can be saved in one tap (iOS & Android)
  • Profile reflects real-time updates made by the user
  • Works offline via cached profile fallback

User Story 2: Team-Wide Brand Consistency & Centralized Control

As a team manager or department lead,

I want to centrally manage and standardize team members’ Wavpe profiles,

So that brand identity, messaging, and compliance are consistent across all external interactions.

Business Value

  • Strengthens brand governance
  • Reduces risk of outdated or incorrect employee information
  • Supports scalable onboarding for growing teams

Acceptance Criteria

  • Admin can create and apply brand templates (logo, colors, disclaimers)
  • Role-based access controls are available (Admin, Manager, User)
  • Bulk profile creation and updates supported
  • Changes reflect instantly across all assigned NFC cards
  • Audit trail available for profile modifications

User Story 3: Enterprise Lead Capture & CRM Integration (B2B Enablement)

As an enterprise sales or business development leader,

I want Wavpe interactions to automatically capture leads and sync with CRM systems,

So that networking activities translate into measurable pipeline outcomes.

Business Value

  • Converts networking into actionable sales intelligence
  • Improves ROI tracking for events and field sales
  • Aligns marketing, sales, and operations data

Acceptance Criteria

  • Every NFC/QR interaction logs a lead with timestamp and location (where permitted)
  • Lead data integrates with CRM platforms (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot)
  • Users can tag interactions (event, meeting, follow-up required)
  • Consent management complies with GDPR and enterprise data policies
  • Admin dashboards show conversion metrics and engagement trends

User Story 4: Advanced Analytics & Engagement Insights

As a product owner or enterprise decision-maker,

I want access to detailed analytics on how and when Wavpe cards are used,

So that I can evaluate adoption, engagement, and business impact.

Business Value

  • Enables data-driven decision-making
  • Demonstrates value to stakeholders and leadership
  • Identifies high-performing teams and use cases

Acceptance Criteria

  • Dashboard displays taps, scans, saves, and follow-ups
  • Analytics segmented by individual, team, region, and time period
  • Exportable reports for PMO and leadership reviews
  • Trend insights and usage benchmarks provided
  • Data refreshes in near real-time

User Story 5: Secure, Scalable, and Enterprise-Ready Platform

As an enterprise IT or compliance stakeholder,

I want Wavpe to meet enterprise-grade security, scalability, and compliance standards,

So that it can be safely deployed across large organizations.

Business Value

  • Accelerates enterprise adoption
  • Reduces procurement and security review friction
  • Builds long-term platform trust

Acceptance Criteria

  • SSO support (SAML / OAuth)
  • Data encryption at rest and in transit
  • Compliance with GDPR, SOC 2, and regional data regulations
  • Ability to deactivate or reassign NFC cards instantly
  • Platform supports thousands of concurrent users without degradation

  1. Edge cases
  • NFC card is already linked to another account
  • User attempts activation with an invalid or damaged NFC card
  • Recipient device does not support NFC
  • Poor internet connectivity during profile access
  • User updates profile while recipient is accessing the profile
  • NFC tap fails and fallback to QR code is required

These scenarios must be handled gracefully to ensure reliability.
